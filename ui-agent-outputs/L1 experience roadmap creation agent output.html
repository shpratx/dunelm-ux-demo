Insights
Insight
Cash shoppers only discover the programme through accidental exposure — no current mechanism proactively recruits the highest-frustration, longest-tenure customer segment. The programme's marketing must lead with the "no credit account required" message to unlock this cohort.
Insight
The plain-English returns data disclosure is not a legal nicety — it is an enrolment gate. Both research participants stated they would not sign up without it. It must appear before the CTA, not in a linked terms document.
Insight
The in-store scan is the highest-friction earn touchpoint and the most likely to be abandoned. A 4-second open-to-scan benchmark must be a technical launch gate. Scan confirmation visible on both customer app and associate POS screen is required to build trust.
Insight
Invisible accumulation is functionally equivalent to no programme at all. Balance visibility on the app home screen — not buried in a sub-menu — is the single highest-impact UX decision for sustained engagement between sale events.
Insight
Customers and associates independently converged on three acceptable POS data points: tier/loyalty status, tenure indicator, points balance. Purchase history at the till crosses into surveillance territory for both user types. This is a precise, researchable design constraint — not a preference.
Insight
Redemption upgrades spend rather than replacing it. M&S credit caused P01 to buy a better coat than planned. Experiential or high-value redemptions (Tesco restaurant deal) drive more engagement than cash-off at checkout. Redemption catalogue design is a behaviour driver, not just a benefit list.
Insight
VIP Sale early access is the programme's most validated mechanic — already proven with nextpay holders. Extending it to loyalty-earned access converts the most powerful retention tool from a credit product to a loyalty product. The "earned access" framing matters: P02 described it as the difference between "a reward for loyalty" and "buy now, pay later."
Insight
The nextpay / online account email split is a confirmed, reproducible failure — not a hypothetical. Account unification must be available as a self-service flow in the loyalty account, surfaced proactively rather than discovered during a failed return.
Insight
The loyalty account QR is the key to solving the returns lookup problem. If the QR functions as a universal customer identifier across all channels, the 12-minute email-guessing return search becomes a 15-second scan. Cross-channel identity is the infrastructure on which all loyalty experience depends.
Insight
Support failures in a loyalty context carry disproportionate emotional weight — a points dispute feels like a broken promise, not just a transaction error. Self-service missed points claims and transparent point reversal logic after returns are essential to maintaining trust in a programme customers have emotionally invested in.
