Next Loyalty Programme — Discussion Guide

C
User Type 1 of 2 Customer — Cash / Debit Shopper (No nextpay Account)
StudyNext Loyalty Programme
Duration60 minutes
MethodModerated Remote Interview
01 Introduction & Consent ~5 min
Moderator Script
Hi [Participant Name], thank you so much for making time to speak with us today — I really appreciate it. My name is [Moderator Name], and I'm a researcher working with the team at [UXReactors / Ascendion]. Joining me today is [Note-taker Name], who will be taking notes so I can focus on our conversation. [If observer present: There may also be one or two colleagues from Next observing quietly — their cameras will be off and they won't be participating in our conversation.]

Before we start, I want to explain what today's session is about. We're speaking with people who shop at Next to understand how they shop and what their experiences are like — particularly around how they feel about being recognised as a customer and the kinds of rewards or programmes that work for them in other contexts. We are not testing any product or design today, and there are absolutely no right or wrong answers. We're here to learn from you, so please feel free to say exactly what you think — including things that might feel critical or negative. That's genuinely the most useful thing you can share.

With your permission, I'd like to record this session so I can focus on our conversation rather than taking notes myself. The recording will only be used by the research team — it won't be shared more widely. Can I confirm you're happy for us to record today?

Do you have any questions before we begin?
Note: Confirm recording consent verbally and via the consent form sent before the session. If participant is not comfortable being recorded, proceed without recording — note-taker leads documentation.
02 Participant Background & Warm-Up ~5 min
To get us started — can you tell me a little bit about yourself and how shopping fits into your week? I'm thinking about things like where you tend to shop, what kinds of things you buy most often, that kind of thing.
Probe: Is shopping something you enjoy, or is it more of a task you get done when you need to?
Probe: How does that split between shopping in-store and shopping online for you?
And when it comes to Next specifically — how would you describe your relationship with Next as a shopper?
Probe: How long have you been shopping there?
Probe: What tends to take you there — is it a regular thing, or more when you have something specific in mind?
03A Shopping Behaviour & What Drives Repeat Visits ~10 min
Maps to Research Goals A1, A2, A4 — FAFRQ: Who is the user? What drives their repeat behaviour?
RQ A1
When you decide to shop at Next rather than somewhere else — what's usually behind that decision?
Probe: Is that the same reason every time, or does it depend on what you're shopping for?
Probe: Has that reason changed over the time you've been shopping there?
Probe: Is there anything that would cause you to go to a competitor instead for something you'd normally buy at Next?
RQ A2
How would you describe the way Next treats you as a customer — do you feel like they know who you are?
Probe: What does that feel like when you're in the store, or when you're on the website?
Probe: Has there ever been a moment where you felt genuinely recognised or appreciated by Next as a customer?
Probe: If you imagine what "being recognised" by a retailer you've shopped at for years would look like — what comes to mind?
RQ A4
Thinking about the way you shop at Next — is there anything a loyalty programme would need to offer for you to actually change what you do? For example, buying something you wouldn't have otherwise, or choosing Next over somewhere else?
Probe: What would that look like in practice?
Probe: Has a loyalty programme ever actually changed where or how much you spend somewhere? Tell me about that.
03B Loyalty Programme Experience at Other Retailers ~12 min
Maps to Research Goals A3, B1–B5 — FAFRQ: What tools and programmes does this user rely on? What makes them engage or lapse?
RQ A3 / B1
Tell me about the loyalty programmes you're currently part of — which ones do you actually use, and how?
Probe: When you think about the one you use most consistently — what is it about that one that keeps you coming back to it?
Probe: Are there any that you signed up for but have basically stopped using? What happened there?
Probe: Has a loyalty programme ever actually changed where you shop — like choosing one place over another specifically to earn points?
RQ B2
When you have points building up in a loyalty programme — how do you keep track of them, and how does that feel?
Probe: What does it feel like when your balance goes up after a purchase?
Probe: Is there a point at which the balance starts to feel like something you'd not want to lose?
Probe: What would make a balance feel unclear or untrustworthy to you?
RQ B3
If you think about the choice between earning points that build over time versus getting a small discount straight away at the checkout — which feels more valuable to you, and why?
Probe: Does that depend on what you're buying or where you're shopping?
Probe: Is there something about saving up for a bigger reward that appeals — or does the wait put you off?
RQ B4
Tell me about the last time you actually used points or a reward from a loyalty programme — what did you use them for, and what was that experience like?
Probe: Did it feel like a good use of them, or did you feel like you could have done better?
Probe: What makes a redemption moment feel worthwhile versus a bit disappointing?
RQ B5
Have you ever been part of a loyalty programme that changed — maybe the earn rate changed, the rewards shifted, or the whole programme was restructured? What happened?
Probe: How did you find out about the change, and what was your reaction?
Probe: What would make a change like that feel acceptable versus like a reason to stop using the programme?
Based on what you've shared about the loyalty programmes you use — I'd like to shift to thinking specifically about what that might look like at Next...
03C Enrolment Drivers, Trust & Data Concerns ~15 min
Maps to Research Goals C1–C5 — FAFRQ: What are the user's biggest problems and concerns? What would unlock engagement?
RQ C1
If Next launched a loyalty programme tomorrow — what would need to be true about it for you to sign up straight away?
Probe: Is there a single thing that would be the deal-maker for you — the thing that made you think, yes, that's worth joining?
Probe: And on the other side — is there anything that would cause you to hesitate or decide not to join?
Probe: If you heard that the early access to the Next sale was available as a loyalty reward rather than needing a credit account — how would that land for you?
RQ C2
What kind of reward would feel most meaningful to you personally — money off, early access to sales, something experiential, or being recognised differently as a customer?
Probe: Does that change depending on what you're buying at Next?
Probe: If you think about the reward that has felt most satisfying from any programme you've been part of — what was it and why did it feel good?
RQ C3
When you think about signing up for a new loyalty programme that would track your purchases — what goes through your mind about your data?
Probe: Are there things you'd be comfortable with them tracking, and things you'd be uncomfortable with?
Probe: Have you heard of anything happening with Next and customer data — returns monitoring or anything like that? What do you know about it, and what do you think about it?
Probe: What would a retailer need to tell you — and where would they need to tell you — for you to feel confident about joining?
RQ C4
If earning and tracking your loyalty points mainly happened through the Next app — what would need to be true about that experience for you to use it regularly?
Probe: What makes you open a loyalty app at a till — and what would make you just reach for your card instead?
Probe: Where in the app would you expect to see your points balance — and how often would you expect it to update?
RQ C5
Is there anyone you can think of in your life — family or friends — who you'd imagine couldn't use a loyalty programme that was app-only?
Probe: Tell me a bit about them — what's their situation with technology and shopping?
Probe: What would they need for a loyalty programme to work for them?
Based on what you've said about how you'd want to join and what matters to you — I'd like to shift now to talk about what actually happens when you're in a Next store...
03D In-Store Recognition & the Point-of-Sale Interaction ~10 min
Maps to Research Goals D1–D3 — FAFRQ: What is the user's journey? Where do the pain points sit in-store?
RQ D1
When you're at the till in a Next store — can you walk me through what that interaction is usually like for you?
Probe: Has there ever been a moment at a till — at Next or anywhere else — where you felt like the person serving you actually knew you were a regular? What was that like?
Probe: If a store associate at Next said "you've got 250 loyalty points — would you like to use them today?" — how would that land for you?
Probe: What would they ideally know about you at the till — and what would feel like too much?
RQ D2
If a store associate could see some information about you on their screen when you scanned your loyalty code — what would you be comfortable with them seeing, and what would you prefer they couldn't see?
Probe: How would you feel if they could see how long you'd been a customer?
Probe: What about your purchase history — what you've bought, or how often you return things?
Probe: Where do you draw the line between feeling recognised and feeling monitored?
RQ D3
If you had to scan your phone app at the till to earn points — what would need to happen for that to feel quick and easy enough that you'd actually do it every time?
Probe: Think of an app you already scan with in-store — how does that process work, and what would you compare it to?
Probe: Is there a point where the queue behind you would make you give up on the scan and just pay?
Probe: What would need to happen on your screen after the scan for you to feel confident it had worked?
Stimulus — Loyalty Programme Concept Card [~3 min]
Moderator instruction: Share screen with the one-page loyalty programme concept summary. Say: "I'd like to show you a summary of what Next's loyalty programme might look like — this is an early concept, nothing is final. I'm interested in your honest reaction — not whether you think it's well designed, just what it makes you think and feel."

Allow participant to read silently for 30 seconds. Then ask:

— What's your overall reaction to this?
— Is there anything here that would make you sign up immediately?
— Is there anything that would give you pause or put you off?
— Is anything missing that you'd expect to see?
04 Wrap-Up ~5 min
We've covered a lot of ground today. Is there anything about your experience as a Next shopper, or your thoughts on loyalty programmes more generally, that we haven't touched on but that you think would be useful for us to know?
If you could give the Next team one piece of advice about what a loyalty programme should do for a customer like you — what would it be?
Closing Script
That's really everything I wanted to cover — thank you so much for your time and for sharing all of that with me. It's been genuinely useful. [Note-taker's name], do you have any follow-up questions?

As a reminder, the recording will only be used by the research team. Your [£50 digital gift card] will be sent to your email address within 5 working days. If you have any questions at all, please feel free to get in touch with [coordinator email]. Thank you again — I really appreciate it.

User Type 2 of 2 Store Associate — Next plc UK Store (POS Role)
StudyNext Loyalty Programme
Duration45 minutes
MethodModerated Remote Interview
01 Introduction & Consent ~5 min
Moderator Script
Hi [Participant Name], thank you so much for joining us today — I really appreciate you making time for this. My name is [Moderator Name], and I'm a researcher working with [UXReactors / Ascendion] on behalf of Next. [Note-taker Name] is joining me and will be taking notes. [If observer: There's also one quiet observer from the Next team — they won't be part of our conversation.]

I want to be clear about what this is and isn't. We're talking to people who work in Next stores because we want to understand what really happens at the till — the real experience, not the ideal version. I'm not here to evaluate your performance or how you do your job. I'm here to learn from your experience, because you know things about how the store actually works that no one sitting in an office does. So please be as honest as you like — the more real and specific you are, the more useful this is for us.

[If consent to record given: With your permission I'd like to record this session — only for the research team's use, never shared more widely. You confirmed this before the session, but I just want to double-check you're still happy with that?]

Any questions before we get started?
Note: This segment requires particular care — associates may feel observed or assessed despite the disclaimer. Reinforce throughout that there are no right answers and their candid perspective is what's needed.
02 Participant Background & Warm-Up ~5 min
Can you start by telling me a bit about your role at Next — how long you've been there, and what a typical shift looks like for you?
Probe: How much of your time is on the till versus on the shop floor?
Probe: Is that split consistent, or does it depend on the day or the season?
What do you enjoy most about working with customers directly — and what do you find most challenging?
Probe: Is there a type of interaction that you feel you're really good at?
03E POS Workflow, Current Systems & Customer Interactions ~18 min
Maps to Research Goals E1–E4 — FAFRQ: Who is this user? What is their workflow? Where are the biggest problems in their day?
RQ E1
Walk me through what happens from the moment a customer comes to your till — what are you managing, step by step?
Probe: At what points in that sequence do you feel like the system is helping you — and at what points does it slow you down or leave you without what you need?
Probe: Is there anything about the way the till system works right now that regularly frustrates you?
Probe: What does the screen show you about the customer you're serving right now?
RQ E2
If you could know one thing about a customer that the till showed you right now — what would it be, and how would it change how you treated them?
Probe: Are there things you'd want to know that you feel confident you'd use in a positive way?
Probe: Are there things about a customer you would actively not want to know when you're serving them — things that might change the interaction in a way you'd be uncomfortable with?
Probe: If you could see that a customer has been shopping at Next for 10 years — what, if anything, would you do differently?
RQ E3
Tell me about a difficult customer interaction you've had at the till — what happened, and how did you handle it?
Probe: Was there a point in that interaction where having more information about the customer's history with Next would have made it easier for you?
Probe: Have you ever been in a situation where a customer was frustrated and it turned out there was context — like previous problems with deliveries or returns — that you had no way of knowing about beforehand? What happened?
Probe: What would have made that interaction go better from your side?
RQ E3 — Returns specifically
Tell me about what happens when someone comes to the till for a return — what does that process look like from your end?
Probe: How do you find the customer's order on the system — what do you use to look it up?
Probe: What happens when you can't find the order? How often does that happen, and how long does it take to resolve?
Probe: What's that experience like for the customer while it's happening — and for you?
RQ E4
When you have a queue building behind a customer — how does that change what you do during the transaction?
Probe: Is there anything you skip or do differently under queue pressure that you'd do more carefully if you had more time?
Probe: What's the point at which you feel you're choosing speed over doing the interaction properly?
Based on what you've told me about the till experience — I'd like to shift to talking about something new that's coming, and what it would mean for you specifically...
03F Loyalty Programme Readiness & Training Needs ~12 min
Maps to Research Goals F1–F4 — FAFRQ: What are this user's biggest problems in adopting new systems? What makes rollouts succeed or fail at the till?
RQ F1
Think back to the last time Next introduced something new that you had to explain to customers at the till — an initiative, a promotion, a new system. What was that experience like?
Probe: How did you find out about it — and did you feel ready to explain it by the time customers started asking?
Probe: Was there a moment where a customer asked you something about it and you weren't sure of the answer? How did you handle that?
Probe: What made that rollout go well or badly from your point of view?
RQ F2
If Next was about to launch a loyalty programme and you needed to be able to explain it to a customer at the till from day one — what kind of preparation would actually make you feel ready?
Probe: What format works best for you — a briefing, a video, something at the start of a shift, something written you can refer back to?
Probe: How much time would you need — and when would it need to happen relative to the launch?
Probe: Is there anything that would make you feel more confident introducing it to a customer mid-transaction, even when there's a queue?
RQ F3
If a loyalty indicator appeared on your till screen when a customer scanned their app — what would you want it to show you, and what would you want it not to show you?
Probe: If you could see three things on screen — just three — what would they be?
Probe: Is there anything about a customer that you'd never want to see on a loyalty screen — information that would make the interaction harder or feel uncomfortable?
Probe: If the screen told you a customer had made a lot of returns — what would you do with that information?
RQ F4
Are there situations where you can imagine it would feel awkward or inappropriate to bring up the loyalty programme with a customer at the till?
Probe: What kind of interaction would make you decide to skip mentioning it entirely?
Probe: How would you want to handle those situations — would you want the flexibility to use your own judgment, or would you prefer a clear script that covers what to do?
04 Wrap-Up ~5 min
Is there anything about what it's actually like to be at that till — or what you'd need from a loyalty programme to make it work from your side — that we haven't covered and that you think would be important for the team to understand?
If you could give the people designing this loyalty programme one piece of advice about what would make it genuinely work at the till — what would it be?
Closing Script
That's everything I wanted to cover — and honestly, what you've shared today is exactly the kind of insight we were hoping to get. Thank you so much for being so open and specific — it makes a real difference. [Note-taker's name], anything to add?
